### [16 July 2026] [08:50] {.log-title}

[Dissociation and **Make** Revelation]{.log-subtitle}

:::

I woke up late. My head hurts. I'm in a state of limbo.
The world around me doesn't feel real anymore. The sounds, the lights,
even the air feel like a synthetic experience.
I often feel like this, especially when I don't have enough sleep
 --- [`[TODO: Find the possible reasons for this and write about it]`]{.todo} --- 
And why don't I have enough sleep this time, you may say. I say
because of that damn Make syntax I can't seem to understand.
All of the concepts seemed to melt together in my head
whenever I try to introduce new targets or rules.
I brain-farted myself all night long.

I think I've come to realize that it is like a recipe --- isn't it obvious ---
a dependency chain. You define a target, and to build that target,
likely you need something, say source files, some list, etc. and then
Make tries to build the target with the rules you specified. I think
that's what I learned tonight.

**Today's takeaway:** Make is going to do only what you tell it to do. 
You tell them recipes. A target declares what you want; 
dependencies declare what the target need; 
and rules describe how to get there.

*I think I'm going to rest for a bit. It's cheat day. I'm going
to light-read and watch some random contents on the Internet 
--- or falls into some rabbit holes on osbscure topics and end up 
collecting endless materials.*
